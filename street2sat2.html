Crop Type Dataset Generated with Helmet‑Mounted GoPros and Deep Learning

Pipeline  |  Resources  |  Publications

Date: August 26, 2025

The Helmets Labeling Crops Project (“Helmets”) develops scalable road‑side image collection to fuel crop‑type mapping. Our Street2Sat platform converts geo‑tagged GoPro images into geo‑referenced, labeled points suitable for pairing with satellite imagery. Explore the Street2Sat GitHub repository. For kit requests: cnakalem@umd.edu.

Presentation & Tutorial Led by

  • Diana Botchway Frimpong, Faculty Specialist – UMD/NASA Harvest

Street2Sat Pipeline

The goal of Street2Sat is to turn geo‑referenced images acquired from roads into geo‑referenced labeled samples for objects of interest. The crop‑type dataset pipeline involves:

  1. GoPros collect road‑side images in agricultural zones – Helmets Data Collection Guide
  2. Collected photos are uploaded to Cloud Storage – Instructions for Uploading
  3. Photos are processed with ML models to produce Google Earth Pro KMZ files – notebooks/1_GoPro2CropKMZ.ipynb
  4. KMZ predictions are verified via QA – Helmets Quality Assessment Instructions
  5. Reviewed KMZ files are converted to CSV – notebooks/2_CropKMZtoCSV.ipynb
